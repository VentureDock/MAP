The Challenge

Why Most International Startups
Fail in the US

Entering the US market is a significant undertaking. These are six of the most common challenges international founders face — and what the program addresses directly.

🎯

Wrong ICP

Assuming your home-market customer profile translates directly into the US.

⏱

Underestimating Sales Cycles

US enterprise sales cycles run 2–4× longer than founders expect from their home markets.

💰

Pricing Off-Benchmark

Setting prices without anchoring to US competitive data — leaving money on the table or pricing yourself out.

⚖️

No Legal Entity

Skipping US incorporation before signing the first customer — a costly mistake that slows everything.

🗺

Treating the US as One Market

The US is 5+ distinct regional markets. Your GTM must reflect that.

🤝

Premature US Hiring

Hiring a VP Sales before achieving problem-solution fit burns cash and credibility fast.

"Most international founders don't fail in the US because they have a bad product. They fail because they have the wrong playbook."
— Venture Dock Program Team

Who It's For

Built for Founders at the Right Stage

Venture Dock is selective by design. We keep groups small (≤10 founders) so every session delivers real value to every person in the room.

  • B2B SaaS or tech-enabled startup

FAQ

Common Questions

What timezone are sessions held in?
Sessions are scheduled to accommodate multiple international time zones. The exact schedule is confirmed after participant selection based on the group's locations. Most sessions run in a US morning / Asia-Europe evening window.
Do I need to be incorporated in the US already?
No. Many founders join before incorporating — we cover the US entity decision in Session 4. However, you should have paying customers or strong early traction before applying.
How much time per week does this require?
Expect 3–5 hours per week: 90 minutes for the live session every two weeks, plus 2–4 hours for homework. Homework is real work — not busywork.
What happens if I miss a session?
Sessions are recorded. Missing one is manageable — missing two is grounds for removal from the program. We enforce attendance because the peer dynamic only works when everyone shows up.
Is Demo Day only for investors?
Demo Day is primarily investor-facing, but you may invite up to 2 guests. The audience is curated and kept small to maximize quality of investor interactions.
What industries do you accept?
We focus on B2B SaaS and tech-enabled services. We do not currently accept B2C consumer products, hardware-only startups, or crypto/web3 projects.
When is the application deadline?
Rolling applications are accepted until the program is full (maximum 10 startups). We recommend applying early as spots fill on a first-accepted basis.

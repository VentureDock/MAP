Why Global Startups
Struggle in the US Market

The US market is full of opportunity, but it is also complex, competitive, and expensive to enter without the right preparation. These are the most common mistakes global founders make — and the exact gaps this program helps solve.

🎯

Wrong ICP

Assuming your home-market customer profile will work in the US without testing buyer behavior, budget, and urgency.

⏱

Underestimating Sales Cycles

Underestimating how long US enterprise sales, procurement, security review, and stakeholder alignment can take.

💰

Pricing Off-Benchmark

Pricing without understanding US benchmarks, buyer expectations, and competitive alternatives.

⚖️

Legal & Operational Readiness

Not being ready for US contracts, entity decisions, tax, compliance, IP, and customer onboarding requirements.

🗺

Treating the US as One Market

Treating the US as one market instead of adapting by industry, region, buyer segment, and channel.

🤝

Premature US Hiring

Hiring too early in the US before validating ICP, messaging, sales motion, and founder-led traction.

FAQ

Common Questions Before You Apply

What timezone are sessions held in?
Sessions are typically scheduled in Pacific Time, with timing selected to support participation from multiple regions. Final session timings will be shared before the cohort begins.
Do I need to be incorporated in the US already?
No. You do not need to be incorporated in the US before joining. One of the sessions will help you understand entity options, legal readiness, and when it makes sense to set up a US presence.
How much time per week does this require?
Expect around 2–3 hours per week, including the live session weeks, homework, and applying the frameworks to your business.
What happens if I miss a session?
Sessions will be recorded where possible, and materials will be shared. However, founders are encouraged to attend live to get the full value from mentor feedback and peer discussion.
Is Demo Day only for investors?
Demo Day is designed to introduce founders to a curated audience of investors, angels, operators, and ecosystem partners.
What industries do you accept?
We primarily accept B2B SaaS, enterprise tech, AI, cybersecurity, deeptech, and tech-enabled startups preparing to enter or expand in the US market.
When is the application deadline?
Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis until the cohort is full. Since seats are limited to up to 20 startups, early applications are encouraged.
Can two people from the same startup attend?
Yes. The $2,000 startup fee includes access for up to two team members from the same company.
Is this program virtual or in person?
The core program is virtual, with six live 90-minute sessions over 12 weeks. Select in-person opportunities may be offered separately based on cohort interest and availability.
Will Venture Dock help with introductions in the US?
Venture Dock gives founders access to relevant mentors, operators, investors, and ecosystem partners. Introductions may be facilitated where there is a clear fit, but the program does not guarantee customer, investor, or funding outcomes.
